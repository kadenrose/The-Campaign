About Mark Hampton

Mark Hampton is a longtime resident of Maricopa County, committed to making local government more responsive and more transparent. Mark prides himself as being an entrepreneur, husband, and father of 5 children. According to Mark, real change starts at the local level.

Mark has served on several community boards in the past where he focused on neighborhood development and safety. His contribution to the county's planning has brought much needed infrastructure improvements to the valley. Mark has a simple approach: listen more, talk less, and always put others first.

Mark was born and raised in Mesa, Arizona into a family of six. His childhood was spent volunteering at his church and joining student council at his schools. He graduated Scottsdale High in 1980, graduated from the University of Arizona in 1984 with a bachelor's in engineering. He married his wife, Sarah in 1982. Started his own business where he and his employees worked contracts with local governments enforcing safety and fire codes.

Mark originally felt called to office in 2022 where he faced trouble expanding his business and encountered firsthand how outdated the local policies were. Motivated to make a difference, Mark left his practice and is running for office 2026.

Issues

  • Growth Management

    Why it matters: Maricopa County continues to grow rapidly, aging our underdeveloped infrastructure faster.

    Mark's stance: As a former engineer and contractor, Mark has experience with government owned and maintained infrastructure. He wants smart, high-quality, long-term plans that ensure roads, utilities, and transportation systems grow along with the community.

    What he'll do: Push for shorter timelines for development and better oversight on projects, support infrastructure impact audits for new residential developments, and improve collaboration with competitive contractors to reduce redundant spending.

  • Support for Local Businesses

    Why it matters: Small business activity is vital to Maricopa County's economy and red tape is holding them stagnant.

    Mark's stance: Based off of his prior experiences, Mark wants the process of starting and expanding a business easier and transparent.

    What he'll do: Streamline permitting and licensing processes, establish small business liaison office, and creating a small business hotline to hear directly from business owners.

  • Public Safety and Emergency Planning

    Why it matters: Safe neighborhoods are a foundation of strong, long-lasting communities, and public safety be proactive not reactive.

    Mark's stance: With experience in enforcing codes, Mark knows how important prevention is and supports the modernization of safety protocols and infrastructure. This will enhance communication between departments and agencies.

    What he'll do: Support better funding for fire prevention and response training, launch neighborhood preparedness programs, work with local departments and agencies to simplify safety codes.

  • Community Development

    Why it matters: As the county expands, older neighborhoods need reinvesting.

    Mark's stance: Every single resident in Maricopa County deserves to feel safe. He believes in interdependent neighborhoods, that support each other.

    What he'll do: Advocate on behalf on property owners for grants to improve existing developments and work with planning committees to preserve neighborhood hospitality.

  • Parks and Amenities

    Why it matters: As a father of five with two dogs, Mark understands the importance of parks and public spaces are for the quality of life in communities.

    Mark's stance: Access to clean, safe, shaded parks should not depend on your ZIP code. Having these spaces will connect communities.

    What he'll do: Increase funding for maintenance and upgrades, propose adding more shaded dog parks that can accommodate the higher temperatures during the summer, and call for community gardens and farmer's markets.
